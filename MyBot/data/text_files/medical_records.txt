=== MEDICAL RECORDS AND PRESCRIPTIONS ===

Medical Records Overview:
Patient medical records include complete health history, consultation notes, prescriptions, diagnostic reports, and appointment history. All records are securely stored and accessible to authorized users.

Prescription Management:
Doctors create prescriptions after consultations. Prescriptions include medication names, dosages, frequency, duration, and special instructions. Patients can view all prescriptions in their medical records.

Prescription History:
All past and current prescriptions are stored in patient records. This helps doctors review medication history and avoid drug interactions during new prescriptions.

Consultation Notes:
Doctors document detailed consultation notes including symptoms, examination findings, diagnosis, treatment plan, and recommendations. These notes are part of the permanent medical record.

Diagnostic Report Integration:
Diagnostic reports are automatically linked to patient records. Doctors can view all test results when reviewing patient history, enabling informed decision-making.

Medical History:
Complete medical history including past illnesses, surgeries, allergies, and chronic conditions is maintained in patient records. This information is crucial for providing appropriate care.

Record Access:
Patients can access their own medical records through the patient dashboard. Doctors can access records of their patients. Diagnostic centers can view relevant test history.

Record Sharing:
Patients can share medical records with doctors or other healthcare providers as needed. Records can be exported or printed for external use.

Privacy and Security:
Medical records are protected with secure authentication. Only authorized personnel can access patient information, ensuring privacy and confidentiality.

Prescription Renewal:
For ongoing medications, patients can request prescription renewals through the system. Doctors review requests and issue renewals as appropriate.

Drug Interaction Checks:
The system helps identify potential drug interactions when doctors prescribe new medications, ensuring patient safety.

Record Updates:
Medical records are updated in real-time. New consultations, prescriptions, and test results are immediately added to patient records.

Long-term Tracking:
Medical records enable long-term tracking of health conditions, treatment effectiveness, and patient progress over time.
